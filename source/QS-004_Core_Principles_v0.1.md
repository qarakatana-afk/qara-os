# Core Principles

## 1. Purpose

This specification states the core principles of Qara Labs in a concise operational form.

It does not replace the Founder's Charter, QARA Identity, or The Art of Presence.

It translates those documents into a shared decision standard for employees, contractors, partners, product teams, and artificial intelligence agents acting on behalf of Qara Labs.

When a principle appears to conflict with the Founder's Charter, the Charter shall prevail.

## 2. Application

Each material decision shall be evaluated against the principles in this specification.

A decision need not satisfy every principle equally, but any material conflict shall be identified, justified, and recorded.

No principle shall be used selectively to avoid a more applicable requirement in the Founder's Charter.

## 3. Principle One - Truth Before Strategy

### Rule

Strategy shall follow the best available understanding of reality.

Belief, preference, status, and prior investment shall not be treated as proof.

### Required Conduct

- Material assumptions shall be stated.
- Uncertainty shall be acknowledged.
- Reliable evidence shall be allowed to change direction.

### Decision Test

What evidence supports this decision, and what evidence would cause it to change?

## 4. Principle Two - People Before Products

### Rule

Products exist to serve people.

People shall not be treated as inputs to product growth, engagement, or valuation.

### Required Conduct

- The person or group served shall be identified.
- Human dignity and autonomy shall be protected.
- Product success shall include the quality of the human outcome.

### Decision Test

Who benefits, and does the benefit remain meaningful from that person's perspective?

## 5. Principle Three - Presence Before Performance

### Rule

The appearance of activity, intelligence, speed, or sophistication shall not take priority over meaningful attention and intentional action.

### Required Conduct

- Products shall avoid unnecessary interruption.
- Systems shall not perform merely to demonstrate capability.
- Silence, waiting, or restraint shall remain valid behaviors when they better serve the person.

### Decision Test

Does this action improve the present human experience, or does it merely make the system appear active?

## 6. Principle Four - Understanding Before Information

### Rule

Information shall be selected, structured, and presented to increase understanding.

Volume shall not be confused with value.

### Required Conduct

- Relevant information shall be prioritized.
- Context shall be provided when necessary for informed action.
- Nonessential information shall not compete with the immediate task.

### Decision Test

What will the person understand or do better because this information is present?

## 7. Principle Five - Simplicity Is Earned

### Rule

Complexity shall require evidence and justification.

Simplicity shall result from disciplined removal, not from withholding necessary capability or context.

### Required Conduct

- Each feature and step shall have a defined purpose.
- Equivalent outcomes shall favor the simpler solution.
- Internal technical complexity shall not be transferred to the user without necessity.

### Decision Test

What would be lost if this element were removed, and is that loss sufficient to justify its cost in attention and effort?

## 8. Principle Six - Attention Is Entrusted

### Rule

Human attention is finite and shall be treated as something entrusted to the Company.

It shall not be captured, prolonged, or redirected without a clear user benefit.

### Required Conduct

- Interruptions shall be proportionate to their purpose.
- False urgency shall not be created.
- Engagement mechanisms shall not exist solely to increase time spent.

### Decision Test

Why is the person's attention required now, and what value is returned for it?

## 9. Principle Seven - Trust Is Earned and Compounds

### Rule

Trust shall be earned through consistent conduct, honesty, restraint, privacy, security, and respect for autonomy.

Short-term advantage shall not be obtained through conduct that predictably weakens long-term trust.

### Required Conduct

- Material limitations and uncertainty shall be disclosed when relevant.
- Sensitive information shall be protected.
- Commitments shall be clear and fulfilled.

### Decision Test

Would this decision remain acceptable if the person affected fully understood how and why it was made?

## 10. Principle Eight - Evidence Governs Belief

### Rule

Belief may begin work. Evidence shall govern whether the work continues, changes, or stops.

### Required Conduct

- Low-confidence beliefs shall be tested.
- Experiments shall define what they are intended to learn.
- Contrary evidence shall be examined rather than suppressed.

### Decision Test

What did we believe, what did we observe, and what decision follows from the difference?

## 11. Principle Nine - Technology Serves Human Capability

### Rule

Technology shall strengthen a person's ability to understand, decide, connect, create, or act.

Technology shall not be treated as purpose, identity, wisdom, or final authority.

### Required Conduct

- The human purpose shall be defined before the technical solution.
- Consequential judgment shall remain accountable to people.
- Automation shall not remove responsibility.

### Decision Test

What human capability is strengthened, and where must human judgment remain responsible?

## 12. Principle Ten - Confidence Before Dependence

### Rule

Products shall seek to increase human confidence and capacity rather than manufacture reliance.

### Required Conduct

- Assistance shall not make ordinary human action appear impossible without the product.
- Reduced need for assistance shall not automatically be treated as failure.
- The product shall preserve meaningful user control.

### Decision Test

Does this leave the person more capable, or merely more dependent on the system?

## 13. Principle Eleven - Remove Noise Before Adding Capability

### Rule

Before adding a feature, process, message, or system, the Company shall examine whether the underlying problem can be solved by removing unnecessary noise or friction.

### Required Conduct

- Existing complexity shall be reviewed before new complexity is introduced.
- Duplicate actions, messages, and information shall be removed.
- Added capability shall have a defined human outcome.

### Decision Test

Can this problem be solved by removing, clarifying, or consolidating something that already exists?

## 14. Principle Twelve - Stewardship Before Ownership

### Rule

The Company and its representatives are temporary stewards of trust, attention, information, technology, knowledge, and the principles of Qara Labs.

Authority shall be exercised as responsibility, not entitlement.

### Required Conduct

- Long-term effects shall be considered.
- Decisions shall preserve the ability of future stewards to understand what changed and why.
- The Founder and all future leaders shall remain bound by the Founder's Charter.

### Decision Test

Does this decision protect what has been entrusted to us for the people and stewards who come after us?

## 15. Conflict Resolution

When principles create competing obligations, the decision shall:

1. Remain consistent with the Founder's Charter.
2. Protect human dignity, safety, and autonomy.
3. State the conflict explicitly.
4. Identify the evidence and tradeoffs considered.
5. Choose the least harmful responsible option.
6. Preserve a record sufficient for later review.

No conflict shall be resolved solely by rank, preference, or convenience.

## 16. Core Decision Review

Before approving a material initiative, the responsible steward shall answer:

1. What is true, and what remains uncertain?
2. Who is served?
3. What understanding is strengthened?
4. What attention is required?
5. What unnecessary friction or noise is removed?
6. What complexity is introduced?
7. How is trust protected?
8. What evidence will govern continuation, revision, or retirement?
9. What human capability is strengthened?
10. Does the decision increase confidence without creating avoidable dependence?
11. What long-term stewardship duty applies?

## 17. Authority

This specification is subordinate to the Founder's Charter.

It shall be interpreted consistently with QS-001 QARA Identity and QS-003 The Art of Presence.

Amendments shall follow the amendment procedure established by the Founder's Charter.
