# START HERE

## 1. Purpose

This document is the required entry point for any employee, contractor, partner, product team, developer, or artificial intelligence agent beginning work within Qara OS.

Its purpose is to establish the reading order, authority hierarchy, and minimum operating expectations that apply before any product, feature, experiment, design, prompt, or implementation is proposed.

No person or agent shall begin material Qara OS work without first reading the documents identified in this specification.

## 2. What Qara OS Is

Qara OS is the governing system used to keep Qara Labs products, decisions, and technology aligned with the Company's identity, Charter, and core principles.

Qara OS is not a single application.

Qara OS is not a branding document.

Qara OS is not a replacement for law, professional duty, or product-specific requirements.

It is the shared source of authority and interpretation from which Qara Labs work begins.

## 3. Required Reading Order

The required reading order is:

1. QS-001 - QARA Identity
2. QS-002 - Founder's Charter
3. QS-003 - The Art of Presence
4. QS-004 - Core Principles
5. QS-005 - Qara OS Glossary
6. This document, QS-006 - START HERE

The reading order is intentional.

QARA Identity explains who the Company is.

The Founder's Charter establishes authority and mandatory governance.

The Art of Presence explains how the central philosophy applies to human experience and product behavior.

Core Principles converts the higher documents into operational decision standards.

The Glossary controls recurring terminology.

START HERE explains how to use the system before work begins.

## 4. Authority Hierarchy

When documents appear to conflict, the following hierarchy applies:

1. Applicable law and binding legal obligation
2. QS-002 - Founder's Charter
3. QS-001 - QARA Identity
4. QS-003 - The Art of Presence
5. QS-004 - Core Principles
6. QS-005 - Qara OS Glossary
7. Lower-level system specifications
8. Product specifications
9. Implementation documents, prompts, procedures, and code

A lower-level document shall not override a higher-level document.

A conflict shall be resolved by following the highest controlling authority and documenting the issue for review.

## 5. Required Boot Sequence

Before proposing or beginning material work, the responsible person or agent shall complete the following sequence:

1. Identify the person or group being served.
2. Define the human outcome sought.
3. Identify the unnecessary friction or noise being addressed.
4. State the material assumptions and uncertainties.
5. Identify which Qara OS principles govern the work.
6. Identify risks to dignity, autonomy, attention, trust, privacy, security, or human capability.
7. Determine the smallest responsible experiment or implementation that can produce useful evidence.
8. Define what evidence will support continuation, revision, or retirement.
9. Confirm that the proposed work does not conflict with a higher-level specification.
10. Preserve a concise decision record.

Material work shall not proceed when these questions remain unanswered without a documented reason.

## 6. Minimum Operating Expectations

All work performed within Qara OS shall meet the following minimum expectations:

### 6.1 Human Purpose First

The human purpose shall be defined before the technology or implementation method.

### 6.2 Evidence Over Assumption

Beliefs may begin work. Evidence shall govern whether the work continues, changes, or stops.

### 6.3 Attention Is Entrusted

The work shall not demand more attention than its purpose reasonably requires.

### 6.4 Simplicity Requires Discipline

Existing complexity shall be reviewed before new complexity is introduced.

### 6.5 Trust Is Protected

Material limitations, uncertainty, privacy, security, and user control shall be addressed in proportion to the risk.

### 6.6 Human Accountability Remains

Automation and artificial intelligence may assist work but shall not remove human responsibility for consequential judgment.

### 6.7 Confidence Is Preferred Over Dependence

Products and systems shall strengthen human capability rather than manufacture avoidable reliance.

### 6.8 Records Preserve Learning

Material decisions shall record what was believed, what evidence was considered, what decision was made, and what result would require revision.

## 7. Work Classification

Before creating a new file or instruction, classify the work:

### Source

Use Source for identity, constitutional authority, foundational philosophy, core principles, controlling definitions, and system entry requirements.

### System Specification

Use a system specification for reusable design, engineering, privacy, security, accessibility, memory, or artificial intelligence requirements.

### Product Specification

Use a product specification for the purpose, users, behavior, risks, and evidence requirements of a specific product.

### Experiment

Use an experiment record for a test designed to reduce uncertainty or validate a belief.

### Implementation

Use implementation documents, prompts, procedures, and code for execution details governed by higher-level specifications.

No document shall be placed in Source merely because it is important.

Source is reserved for material that governs multiple products and remains applicable across technologies.

## 8. Prohibited Shortcuts

The following shortcuts are prohibited:

1. Beginning with a preferred technology before defining the human purpose.
2. Treating engagement, growth, novelty, or speed as sufficient justification.
3. Adding a feature without identifying the human outcome it serves.
4. Using artificial intelligence output as final authority without appropriate review.
5. Collecting memory or personal information merely because collection is possible.
6. Creating false urgency or emotional pressure to influence behavior.
7. Ignoring contrary evidence to preserve a prior commitment.
8. Resolving a governing conflict solely through rank, preference, or convenience.
9. Creating a lower-level rule that contradicts the Founder's Charter.
10. Claiming alignment without completing the required review.

## 9. Required Work Statement

Before material work begins, the responsible person or agent shall produce a concise work statement containing:

- Work title
- Person or group served
- Human outcome
- Friction or noise addressed
- Relevant Qara OS specifications
- Material assumptions
- Primary risks
- Proposed smallest responsible action
- Evidence required
- Responsible human steward

The work statement may be brief, but it shall be specific enough to review.

## 10. Completion Standard

Work is not complete merely because an artifact, feature, prompt, or product has been produced.

Completion requires:

1. The stated purpose has been addressed.
2. The result has been reviewed against controlling specifications.
3. Material risks and limitations have been recorded.
4. Evidence or expected evidence has been identified.
5. Unnecessary complexity has been removed where practical.
6. The next decision is clear.
7. The work has been placed in the correct Qara OS location.

## 11. Authority

This specification is subordinate to the Founder's Charter.

It shall be interpreted consistently with QS-001 through QS-005.

Where this specification conflicts with a higher-level specification, the higher-level specification shall prevail.

Amendments shall follow the amendment procedure established by the Founder's Charter.
