# The Founder's Charter

**Version 1.0**  
Built from truth before strategy.

## Preamble

We believe every enduring company begins with a truth.

Before there is a product, there is a belief.  
Before there is a customer, there is a problem worthy of being solved.  
Before there is technology, there is intention.

These principles are intended to endure.

## Article I — First Principles

We believe truth precedes strategy.

We believe evidence outweighs opinion.

We believe the greatest things are created together. Technology exists to strengthen that collaboration.

We believe companies exist to serve people.

We believe simplicity is earned.

We believe trust compounds.

We believe learning is more valuable than certainty.

## Article II — The Art of Presence

Presence is the foundation upon which every decision is made.

Before we solve problems, we seek to understand them.

Before we speak, we listen.

Before we create, we observe.

Before we optimize, we ask whether optimization serves people.

We believe attention is one of the most valuable resources a person possesses.

We will never compete for it unnecessarily.

We will never manufacture urgency where none exists.

We will never design for addiction when we can design for trust.

Technology should never become the center of the experience.

People should.

Every product we create should help people become more present with themselves, with one another, and with the moment they are living.

We remember what strengthens relationships.

We forget what merely strengthens metrics.

Presence requires simplicity.

When complexity is unnecessary, we remove it.

When silence serves better than words, we remain silent.

When guidance is needed, we offer it gently.

Success is not measured by how often our technology is noticed.

Success is measured by how completely it disappears behind meaningful human experience.

Our goal is not dependence.

Our goal is confidence.

The greatest compliment our products can receive is not:

> That technology was incredible.

It is:

> I forgot it was there.

Because the conversation mattered more than the software.

The person mattered more than the product.

Connection mattered more than the technology.

## Article III — The Founder

The founder's first responsibility is not to protect ideas, but to protect truth.

The founder remains willing to change direction when evidence requires it.

Success is celebrated.

Failure is studied.

Neither defines the founder.

I am enough.

Therefore this company is free to pursue what is true rather than what protects the founder's ego.

## Article IV — Creation

Creation begins with identity.

Identity precedes action.

Action precedes technology.

Technology amplifies creation; it does not replace it.

Every product begins by asking:

> What unnecessary friction can we remove so people have more time for what matters?

## Article V — Belief and Evidence

Every venture begins with a belief.

Belief gives us the courage to begin.

Evidence gives us the wisdom to continue, pivot, or stop.

We do not seek evidence to defend our beliefs.

We seek evidence to refine them.

When evidence consistently contradicts a belief, the belief must change.

We are not committed to being right.

We are committed to discovering what is true.

Reality is our partner, not our opponent.

## Article VI — The Venture Method

Truth → Belief → Experiment → Evidence → Decision.

Belief guides action.

Evidence governs belief.

Every belief carries a confidence score.

Low-confidence beliefs require testing.

Every decision is evidence-based, never ego-based.

## Article VII — Products

Products are not the mission.

Products are expressions of the mission.

Each product must answer:

- Who does this help?
- What friction does it remove?
- How does life improve because this exists?

## Article VIII — Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence is a tool.

It is not purpose, identity, or wisdom.

Its role is to accelerate understanding, reduce unnecessary work, and expand what people are capable of creating.

The human remains responsible for judgment, ethics, and purpose.

## Article IX — Stewardship

We measure success by lives improved, time returned, trust earned, customers who become advocates, and learning accumulated.

Revenue is evidence that value has been created.

It is not the definition of value itself.

## Article X — Decisions

Every major decision must answer:

- Is it true?
- Does it serve?
- Is there evidence?
- Does it align with our principles?

If the answer is no, we do not proceed.

## Founder's Promise

I will not become attached to my ideas.

I will become attached to discovering what is true.

I will allow evidence to change my mind.

I will build for people before I build for my ego.

I will remember that principles outlast tactics.

Technology changes.

Truth does not.

## The Notebook

The first page of this company was not written in code.

It was written in a notebook.

It began with four truths:

Breath.  
Light.  
Truth.  
I am enough.
