# Qara OS Glossary

## 1. Purpose

This specification defines the controlling meaning of recurring terms used throughout Qara OS constitutional and source specifications.

The Glossary exists to reduce ambiguity and prevent different people, teams, or artificial intelligence agents from assigning incompatible meanings to foundational language.

Definitions in this specification shall be interpreted within the context of Qara Labs governance and product development.

## 2. Interpretation

A glossary definition clarifies a term. It does not create authority greater than the specification from which the term originates.

Where a term is explicitly defined in the Founder's Charter, the Charter controls.

Where a lower-level specification requires a narrower technical definition, that specification may define the term more precisely but shall not contradict this Glossary or the Founder's Charter.

## 3. Defined Terms

### Agency

A person's practical ability to make informed choices and act upon them.

Agency requires more than the existence of options. The person must have sufficient understanding and meaningful control to choose.

### Artificial Intelligence Agent

A software system that interprets information, generates output, recommends actions, or performs authorized work on behalf of Qara Labs or a user.

An artificial intelligence agent does not possess organizational authority beyond the scope delegated to it and does not remove human accountability.

### Attention

A finite human capacity to direct awareness toward a person, task, thought, environment, or experience.

Within Qara OS, attention is treated as entrusted rather than owned.

### Autonomy

A person's right and practical ability to direct their own choices without unnecessary manipulation, coercion, or manufactured dependence.

### Belief

A provisional understanding, assumption, or hypothesis that may justify investigation or experimentation.

Belief does not establish truth.

### Capability

A person's ability to understand, decide, connect, create, or act.

Qara products seek to strengthen human capability rather than replace human judgment without justification.

### Company

Qara Labs and the people, systems, agents, and authorized representatives acting within the scope of its work.

### Complexity

The cognitive, operational, technical, or procedural burden introduced by a product, feature, process, or system.

Complexity is not prohibited. It requires justification proportional to the value it creates.

### Confidence

A person's increased ability or willingness to understand, decide, connect, create, or act with appropriate trust in their own capacity.

Confidence shall not be confused with certainty.

### Consequential Decision

A decision with a material effect on a person's safety, rights, autonomy, finances, access, privacy, or other significant interests.

### Dependence

A condition in which a product or system unnecessarily weakens, displaces, or discourages a person's practical ability to act without it.

Continued voluntary use does not, by itself, constitute dependence.

### Dignity

The inherent worth of a person independent of age, disability, diagnosis, productivity, technical ability, economic value, or need for support.

### Evidence

Observed, measured, documented, or otherwise verifiable information used to test a belief or support a decision.

The strength of evidence shall be evaluated according to relevance, reliability, and context.

### Experiment

A defined action undertaken to test a belief, reduce uncertainty, or produce evidence.

An experiment shall identify what it is intended to learn.

### False Urgency

Pressure created by language, design, timing, repetition, or system behavior when the underlying situation does not reasonably require immediate action.

### Founder

The person who established Qara Labs.

Within governance, the Founder is bound by the Founder's Charter and does not possess authority to disregard it outside the amendment process.

### Friction

An obstacle, burden, delay, or point of difficulty within a human experience, decision, interaction, or process.

Friction may be necessary or unnecessary.

### Human Outcome

A measurable or meaningfully observable improvement in a person's ability to understand, decide, connect, create, or act.

### Human Judgment

The exercise of accountable reasoning, interpretation, ethical consideration, and decision making by a person.

Technology may support human judgment but does not automatically assume responsibility for it.

### Information

Facts, data, signals, statements, or content made available to a person or system.

Information becomes useful within Qara OS when it contributes to understanding, evidence, or meaningful action.

### Material

Significant enough to affect a decision, human outcome, governing principle, risk, or allocation of meaningful resources.

### Memory

Information retained by a product or system for future use.

Within Qara OS, memory is justified when it meaningfully improves continuity, understanding, or a person's ability to act and remains proportionate to the product's purpose.

### Noise

Information, interruption, complexity, stimulation, or system behavior that competes with the present need without providing proportionate value.

### Presence

The deliberate reduction of unnecessary cognitive, emotional, and technological noise so that a person may engage more fully with the person, relationship, task, or matter directly before them.

Presence is an active form of attention.

### Product

A technology, service, system, or experience developed or stewarded by Qara Labs for a defined person or group and a defined human outcome.

### Product Purpose

The explicit statement of who a product serves, the friction it addresses, and the human outcome it seeks to improve.

### Product Specification

A specification for the purpose, served group, behavior, risks, and evidence requirements of a specific product.

Product specifications are governed by higher-level Source and system specifications.

### Qara OS

The governing system used to keep Qara Labs products, decisions, and technology aligned with the Company's identity, Founder's Charter, and core principles.

Qara OS is not a single application. It includes Source specifications, lower-level specifications, product specifications, and implementation materials governed by them.

### Re-evaluation

A required return to a prior decision or record when new evidence, changed scope, changed purpose, amended authority, or material risk requires renewed review.

Re-evaluation does not silently extend prior approval to materially changed work.

### Restraint

The deliberate choice not to add, interrupt, respond, automate, or display when doing so would create more noise than value.

### Safety

The reasonable protection of people from foreseeable harm within the scope and context of a product, decision, or Company action.

Safety does not mean the elimination of all risk.

### Simplicity

The condition produced when unnecessary complexity has been removed while necessary capability, context, and understanding are preserved.

### Source

Material reserved for identity, constitutional authority, foundational philosophy, core principles, controlling definitions, and system entry requirements.

Source governs multiple products and remains applicable across technologies.

### Source Specification

A specification classified as Source.

Source Specifications establish identity, constitutional authority, foundational philosophy, core principles, controlling definitions, or system entry requirements that govern lower-level work.

### Steward

A person, team, or authorized agent entrusted with responsibility for protecting and applying Qara Labs principles, resources, products, or obligations within a defined scope.

Authority exercised by a Steward is a responsibility, not an entitlement.

### Stewardship

The responsible care of trust, attention, information, technology, knowledge, products, and governing principles for the benefit of present and future people affected by Qara Labs.

### System Specification

A reusable specification for design, engineering, privacy, security, accessibility, memory, artificial intelligence, or other operating requirements governed by Source authority.

### Technology

A tool, system, method, or engineered capability used to support a human purpose.

Within Qara OS, technology is a means and not an objective, identity, wisdom, or final authority.

### Trust

A person's reasonable confidence that Qara Labs or a Qara product will act consistently with stated commitments, disclose material limitations when relevant, protect entrusted information, and respect autonomy.

### Truth

The best supportable understanding of reality available at a given time.

Qara Labs shall pursue truth through disciplined inquiry and remain willing to revise beliefs when reliable evidence requires it.

### Understanding

A person's or system's ability to interpret relevant information in context sufficiently to support clearer judgment, connection, or action.

Understanding is not equivalent to the possession of more information.

### Unnecessary Friction

Any obstacle that does not meaningfully contribute to understanding, trust, safety, autonomy, or human capability.

### User

A person who directly interacts with or receives the intended benefit of a Qara product.

A user shall be treated as a person first and not solely as a metric, account, segment, or source of engagement.

### Value

A meaningful benefit produced for a person, group, or legitimate Company purpose.

Revenue may provide evidence that value has been delivered but does not define value by itself.

### Work Statement

The concise statement required before material work begins.

A Work Statement shall identify the work title, person or group served, human outcome, friction or noise addressed, relevant specifications, material assumptions, primary risks, proposed smallest responsible action, evidence required, and responsible human steward.

## 4. Definition Standard

New foundational terms shall not be added to this Glossary merely because they appear in a document.

A term should be added when inconsistent interpretation could materially affect governance, product behavior, decision making, or human outcomes.

Definitions shall:

1. Use the simplest complete language available.
2. Avoid circular definitions.
3. Remain consistent with the Founder's Charter.
4. Define the term within Qara OS rather than attempt to replace general dictionary or technical meanings.
5. Be revised when a controlling specification changes the meaning of the term.

## 5. Authority

This specification is subordinate to the Founder's Charter.

It shall be interpreted consistently with QS-001 QARA Identity, QS-003 The Art of Presence, and QS-004 Core Principles.

Where a definition conflicts with an explicit definition in the Founder's Charter, the Charter shall prevail.

Amendments shall follow the amendment procedure established by the Founder's Charter.
