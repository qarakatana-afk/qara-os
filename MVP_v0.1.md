# QARA OS v0.1 MVP Definition

## One-Line MVP Statement

**QARA OS v0.1 demonstrates that the Kernel can consistently guide an AI through a structured, transparent, and auditable decision-making process that produces repeatable recommendations grounded in its governing authority rather than ad hoc reasoning.**

---

## Purpose

The objective of v0.1 is not to build the complete QARA OS ecosystem. It is to validate the core premise that every recommendation generated by the system can be traced back to an explicit decision process governed by the Kernel.

Success is measured by the reliability of the decision framework—not by the number of features, interfaces, or agents.

This release intentionally limits scope to proving that the Kernel functions as the authoritative execution engine for QARA OS.

---

# Measurable Outcomes

## Outcome 1 — Complete Kernel Execution

Every recommendation generated by the prototype follows the required Kernel execution protocol without skipping mandatory stages.

### Success Criteria

* 100% of prototype recommendations include a complete Kernel execution trace.
* Every required decision stage is executed in the defined authority order.
* Any escalation or exception is explicitly documented.

---

## Outcome 2 — Repeatable Recommendations

When presented with the same inputs under the same governing documents, the system produces substantially consistent recommendations.

### Success Criteria

* A minimum of five identical test runs are performed for selected scenarios.
* At least 80% of the resulting recommendations are judged **equivalent**.

Two recommendations are considered **equivalent** if they:

1. Reach the same final recommendation or decision.
2. Follow the same required Kernel execution stages in the prescribed authority order.
3. Reference the same governing authorities or an equivalent set of authorities that support the decision.
4. Do not introduce contradictory assumptions or materially different reasoning that would change the outcome.

Minor differences in wording, formatting, explanatory detail, or writing style do **not** affect equivalence.

Any recommendation that produces a different outcome, relies on different governing authority without justification, skips required Kernel stages, or materially changes the reasoning path is considered **non-equivalent** and must be documented in the corresponding Decision Record.

---

## Outcome 3 — Auditable Decision Records

Every recommendation automatically generates a Decision Record that allows another reviewer to understand how the conclusion was reached.

### Success Criteria

Each Decision Record includes:

* Original inputs
* Governing documents consulted
* Assumptions made
* Kernel execution summary
* Confidence assessment
* Escalation status (if applicable)
* Final recommendation

No recommendation is considered complete without its accompanying Decision Record.

---

# Definition of Success

QARA OS v0.1 is considered successful if it demonstrates that the Kernel can consistently produce transparent, repeatable, and auditable recommendations that adhere to its governing authority.

The goal of this release is to validate the integrity of the decision-making process itself. Additional capabilities—including specialized agents, memory systems, orchestration, and user interfaces—are intentionally deferred until the Kernel has been proven reliable through measurable evidence.
