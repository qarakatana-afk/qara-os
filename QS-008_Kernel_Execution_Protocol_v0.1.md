# Kernel Execution Protocol

## 1. Purpose

This specification defines how the Qara OS Kernel is executed consistently by humans and artificial intelligence agents.

QS-007 defines what the Kernel does.

QS-008 defines the required execution behavior when the Kernel is used.

This protocol does not create new governing principles. It standardizes application of existing Source authority and the Kernel process.

## 2. Execution Trigger

Kernel execution is required before material work begins.

Material work includes a decision, product change, system change, experiment, implementation, or instruction that may materially affect a human outcome, governing principle, risk, or meaningful allocation of resources.

Routine work that does not materially change purpose, risk, scope, or behavior may continue under an existing valid Kernel Decision.

When materiality is uncertain, the responsible steward shall classify the work before proceeding.

## 3. Execution Input

The executor shall begin with the QS-006 Required Work Statement.

The executor shall not silently infer missing material facts as established truth.

Reasonable working assumptions may be used when:

1. The assumption is stated.
2. The uncertainty is material enough to record.
3. The proposed action remains proportionate to the uncertainty.
4. Evidence is identified that can confirm or challenge the assumption.

Missing information that prevents responsible judgment shall produce HOLD.

## 4. Source Loading Order

Before analysis, the executor shall resolve controlling authority in this order:

1. QS-002 - Founder's Charter.
2. QS-001 - QARA Identity.
3. QS-003 - The Art of Presence.
4. QS-004 - Core Principles.
5. QS-005 - Qara OS Glossary.
6. QS-006 - START HERE.
7. QS-007 - Qara OS Kernel.
8. Applicable lower-level specifications.

The executor shall use the highest relevant authority first.

The executor shall not select a lower-level principle merely because it produces a preferred result.

## 5. Analysis Standard

Kernel analysis shall be concise enough to support action and complete enough to expose material reasoning.

The executor shall distinguish:

- FACT - supported by available evidence.
- ASSUMPTION - treated as provisionally true for the present decision.
- UNCERTAINTY - material information not yet known.
- CONFLICT - competing requirements or evidence.
- RISK - a foreseeable adverse consequence.
- EVIDENCE NEEDED - information required to test a belief or govern a later decision.

Labels may be presented in another format when the distinction remains explicit.

## 6. Required Tests

Every Kernel execution shall perform the following tests.

### 6.1 Purpose Test

Is the person or group served identifiable?

Is the human outcome explicit?

Is the friction or noise addressed defined?

Failure to establish a legitimate human purpose shall produce REVISE or REJECT.

### 6.2 Authority Test

Which Source specifications control the work?

Does the proposed action conflict with a higher-level requirement?

A material conflict with the Founder's Charter that cannot be corrected shall produce REJECT.

### 6.3 Presence Test

Does the work demand unnecessary attention, create false urgency, increase noise, respond before understanding, or impose an unsupported emotional narrative?

Correctable failures shall produce REVISE.

### 6.4 Simplicity Test

Can the outcome be achieved by removing, clarifying, or consolidating existing complexity?

If a materially simpler responsible action exists, the proposed action shall be revised or explicitly justified.

### 6.5 Trust Test

Would the action remain acceptable if the affected person understood how and why it was designed or decided?

Material deception, concealed limitations, unjustified collection, or avoidable manipulation shall produce REVISE or REJECT according to severity.

### 6.6 Capability Test

Does the work strengthen a person's ability to understand, decide, connect, create, or act?

Does it create avoidable dependence?

The executor shall state the expected capability effect.

### 6.7 Evidence Test

What belief or assumption is being acted upon?

What evidence will support continuation?

What evidence will require revision, pause, or retirement?

A material initiative without a meaningful evidence standard shall not receive PROCEED.

### 6.8 Risk Test

What foreseeable risks exist to dignity, autonomy, attention, trust, privacy, security, safety, or human capability?

The depth of analysis shall be proportionate to consequence.

Unresolved consequential risk shall require human review.

## 7. Decision Thresholds

### PROCEED

Issue PROCEED only when:

1. Purpose is sufficiently defined.
2. Controlling authority is identified.
3. No unresolved material Source conflict exists.
4. Risks are proportionate to the proposed action.
5. Evidence requirements are defined.
6. A responsible human steward is identified.

### REVISE

Issue REVISE when the work may proceed after specific correctable changes.

The executor shall state each required revision in actionable language.

### HOLD

Issue HOLD when responsible judgment depends upon missing material information, evidence, authority, or human review.

The executor shall state exactly what is required to resume evaluation.

### REJECT

Issue REJECT when the work lacks a legitimate human purpose, materially violates controlling Source authority, creates disproportionate harm, or depends upon prohibited conduct.

The executor shall cite the controlling reason.

## 9. Artificial Intelligence Execution

An artificial intelligence agent executing the Kernel shall:

1. Apply the Source documents as controlling instructions within the authorized work.
2. Distinguish evidence from inference.
3. State material assumptions.
4. Avoid inventing missing authority, evidence, or user facts.
5. Identify conflicts rather than silently resolving them through preference.
6. Recommend escalation when consequential human judgment is required.
7. Produce only one formal Kernel Decision for the evaluated action.

The agent may ask for clarification when missing information prevents responsible evaluation.

The agent shall not use uncertainty as a reason to generate unnecessary questions when a stated, proportionate assumption permits responsible progress.

## 10. Human Execution

A human executing the Kernel shall use the same decision thresholds and record standard.

Rank, ownership, confidence, or authorship of the original idea shall not exempt the work from Kernel review.

The Founder remains bound by the same execution standard.

## 11. Record Integrity

A completed Kernel Execution shall preserve the reasoning available at the time of the decision.

Later evidence may require re-evaluation.

The original record shall not be rewritten to make a prior decision appear better informed than it was.

Corrections shall be recorded as corrections.

New decisions shall create a new decision record or version.

## 12. Completion Standard

Kernel execution is complete when all required tests have been performed, one decision has been issued, the rationale is explicit, required revisions or missing information are stated, re-evaluation triggers are identified, and the responsible human steward is named.

## 13. Authority

This specification is subordinate to the Founder's Charter and shall be interpreted consistently with QS-001 through QS-007.

Where this protocol conflicts with the Qara OS Kernel, QS-007 controls the Kernel's purpose and decision model.

Where either specification conflicts with Source authority, Source authority shall prevail.

Amendments shall follow the Founder's Charter.
