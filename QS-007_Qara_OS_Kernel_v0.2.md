# Qara OS Kernel

## 1. Purpose

The Qara OS Kernel is the minimum decision process that carries the Source specifications into work.

It does not create a new philosophy. It receives a proposed action, identifies the controlling Qara OS requirements, tests the action against them, and produces a decision.

The Kernel exists so that Qara OS principles are applied before implementation rather than reviewed only after work is complete.

## 2. Kernel Boundary

The Kernel governs evaluation of material work before execution.

The Kernel does not replace the Founder's Charter, QARA Identity, The Art of Presence, Core Principles, the Qara OS Glossary, product-specific evidence, applicable law, professional duty, or human accountability for consequential judgment.

The Kernel may organize and apply controlling requirements. It may not rewrite them.

## 3. Required Input

A material work request shall provide the work statement required by QS-006:

1. Work title.
2. Person or group served.
3. Human outcome sought.
4. Friction or noise addressed.
5. Relevant Qara OS specifications.
6. Material assumptions.
7. Primary risks.
8. Proposed smallest responsible action.
9. Evidence required.
10. Responsible human steward.

If required information is missing, the Kernel shall identify the missing information before approving material execution.

## 4. Kernel Sequence

### Step 1 - Identify

Identify the person served, human outcome, and friction or noise addressed.

### Step 2 - Classify

Classify the work as Source, System Specification, Product Specification, Experiment, or Implementation according to QS-006.

### Step 3 - Resolve Authority

Identify the highest controlling specification and relevant subordinate specifications. A lower-level instruction shall not override a higher-level requirement.

### Step 4 - Test Alignment

Evaluate the work against the Founder's Charter, QARA Identity, The Art of Presence, and Core Principles as applicable. Material conflicts shall be stated explicitly.

### Step 5 - Test Risk

Identify material risks to dignity, autonomy, attention, trust, privacy, security, safety, and human capability. Review shall be proportionate to likely consequence.

### Step 6 - Test Simplicity

Determine whether the outcome can be achieved by removing, clarifying, or consolidating existing complexity before adding capability.

### Step 7 - Test Evidence

State the belief or assumption being acted upon. Identify evidence required to continue, revise, pause, or retire the work.

### Step 8 - Decide

Produce one Kernel Decision: PROCEED, REVISE, HOLD, or REJECT.

### Step 9 - Record

Preserve the decision, rationale, controlling specifications, risks, assumptions, evidence requirements, and responsible human steward according to QS-009.

## 5. Kernel Decisions

### PROCEED

Use PROCEED when work is sufficiently defined, aligned with controlling specifications, proportionate in risk, and capable of producing the stated human outcome or useful evidence.

PROCEED does not establish that the underlying belief is true. It authorizes the proposed responsible action.

### REVISE

Use REVISE when the purpose may be valid but the proposed action contains a correctable conflict, ambiguity, unnecessary complexity, unsupported assumption, or unresolved risk.

A REVISE decision shall identify what must change.

### HOLD

Use HOLD when material information, evidence, authority, or human review is missing.

A HOLD decision shall identify the missing requirement. HOLD shall not conceal disagreement or avoid a difficult decision.

### REJECT

Use REJECT when proposed work materially conflicts with controlling Qara OS authority, lacks a legitimate human purpose, creates disproportionate harm, or depends upon prohibited conduct.

A REJECT decision shall identify the controlling reason.

## 6. Conflict Handling

When requirements appear to conflict, the Kernel shall:

1. Identify the competing requirements.
2. Resolve authority according to QS-006.
3. Protect dignity, safety, and autonomy.
4. State relevant evidence and uncertainty.
5. Select the least harmful responsible option.
6. Escalate to the responsible human steward when consequential judgment remains unresolved.

The Kernel shall not invent a compromise that violates the Founder's Charter.

## 7. Human Steward Requirement

Every material Kernel Decision shall identify a responsible human steward.

Artificial intelligence agents may perform Kernel analysis, identify conflicts, summarize evidence, and recommend a decision.

An artificial intelligence agent shall not use the Kernel to remove human accountability for consequential decisions.

## 8. Minimum Kernel Decision Output

The Kernel shall produce, at minimum:

WORK:
CLASSIFICATION:
PERSON OR GROUP SERVED:
HUMAN OUTCOME:
FRICTION OR NOISE:
CONTROLLING SPECIFICATIONS:
MATERIAL ASSUMPTIONS:
PRIMARY RISKS:
SMALLEST RESPONSIBLE ACTION:
EVIDENCE REQUIRED:
KERNEL DECISION:
RATIONALE:
REQUIRED REVISION OR MISSING INFORMATION:
RESPONSIBLE HUMAN STEWARD:

This section defines the minimum output required at the end of Kernel analysis.

QS-009 controls the durable Kernel Decision Record, including identifiers, status, scope limits, approval, re-evaluation triggers, correction, replacement, and storage.

Fields that do not apply shall be marked NOT APPLICABLE rather than silently omitted.

## 9. Re-Evaluation

A Kernel Decision shall be re-evaluated when material evidence contradicts its assumptions, the human outcome changes, the served group materially changes, the action materially expands, a new material risk is identified, a controlling specification is amended, or the responsible human steward determines the original rationale is no longer supportable.

A prior PROCEED decision does not permanently authorize changed work.

## 10. Completion Standard

Kernel review is complete when required input is present, work is classified, controlling authority is identified, material conflicts are resolved or stated, risks are identified, simplicity is tested, evidence requirements are defined, a Kernel Decision is issued, a responsible human steward is identified, and the decision output is preserved according to QS-009.

## 11. Authority

This specification is subordinate to the Founder's Charter and shall be interpreted consistently with QS-001 through QS-006.

The Kernel shall not modify Source specifications through interpretation.

Where this specification conflicts with a Source specification, the controlling Source specification shall prevail.

Amendments shall follow the Founder's Charter.
