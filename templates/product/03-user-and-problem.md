## Purpose of This Document

This document defines who this product is built for and the specific problem it solves in their life. It must be precise enough to drive design decisions and reject scope that does not serve this user.

---

## 1. Primary User

Describe the primary user in concrete terms. Avoid marketing personas. Use real circumstances.

```
Who they are: [Age range, life stage, relevant context — not demographic labels]

What they are trying to do: [The job, goal, or need they have right now]

What they currently use to address this: [Existing tools, habits, workarounds]

What is inadequate about what they currently use: [The gap this product fills]
```

---

## 2. Secondary Users

Are there other people who interact with this product in a supporting role (caregivers, facilitators, administrators, partners)?

```
Secondary user: [Brief description]
Their relationship to the primary user: [How they interact with or through the product]
Needs that must not be met at the expense of the primary user: [Be specific]
```

If no secondary users, write: None identified.

---

## 3. Who This Product Is NOT For

State explicitly who this product is not designed to serve. This prevents feature pressure from the wrong users.

```
- Not for: [User type or scenario — and why]
- Not for: [User type or scenario — and why]
```

## 5. Root Cause

What underlying condition creates this problem? Addressing the root cause is more durable than treating symptoms.

```
Root cause: [In 1–3 sentences]
```

---

## 6. Context of Use

Where, when, and in what emotional state does this person typically encounter this problem?

```
Where: [Physical or digital context]
When: [Time of day, life event, emotional state]
Emotional state: [Anxious / Hopeful / Distracted / Overwhelmed / Other]
Constraints: [Phone only / Low connectivity / Limited time / Other]
```

---

## 7. Success from the User's Perspective

In the user's own words (or language close to theirs), what does success look like? Not your success metric — theirs.

```
"I would know this is working if: [User's own words or close approximation]"
```

- [ ] Problem is stated from the user's perspective, not the solution's
- [ ] Evidence is labeled (fact, inference, assumption, uncertain)
- [ ] Root cause identified
- [ ] Context of use is specific enough to inform design
- [ ] Non-users are explicitly excluded
