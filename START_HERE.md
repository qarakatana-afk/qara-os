## What is Qara OS?

**Qara OS** is the operating system for building products that prioritize human connection over technology.

It is a living set of principles, specifications, and frameworks that govern how Qara Labs products are discovered, designed, built, and evaluated.

> *Products change. Technology changes. The principles remain.*

In practical terms: before any new product, feature, or system is built at Qara Labs, it passes through Qara OS. Not as bureaucracy — as a discipline of care.

## Boot Sequence

Before proposing or beginning material work, answer these questions:

1. Who is the person or group being served?
2. What is the human outcome sought?
3. What unnecessary friction or noise is being addressed?
4. What are the material assumptions and uncertainties?
5. Which Qara OS principles govern the work?
6. What risks to dignity, autonomy, attention, trust, privacy, or security exist?
7. What is the smallest responsible action that produces useful evidence?
8. What evidence will support continuation, revision, or retirement?

> This is the Qara OS Boot Sequence. Full details are in [docs/philosophy/06-Start_Here_Specification.md](docs/philosophy/06-Start_Here_Specification.md).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is this a code repository?**
A: Not primarily. Qara OS is a documentation and principles repository. It is the foundation from which code repositories inherit their direction.

**Q: Can I use these principles in my own projects?**
A: See [LICENSE.md](LICENSE.md) for usage terms.

**Q: How are decisions made here?**
A: Through the Kernel process. See [docs/kernel/](docs/kernel/) for the full decision process.

**Q: How do I propose a change to a foundational document?**
A: See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for the amendment process.

**Q: Where are the original specification files?**
A: Original source specifications are in the [source/](source/) folder. Renamed copies with descriptive filenames are in [docs/philosophy/](docs/philosophy/).
