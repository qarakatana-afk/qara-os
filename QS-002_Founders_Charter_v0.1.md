# Qara OS Constitutional Specification
## Founder's Charter

## Preamble

Qara Labs exists to discover, develop, and steward products that reduce unnecessary friction in human experience through disciplined inquiry, evidence-based decision making, and technology that strengthens human understanding.

The Company recognizes that products, technologies, markets, and leadership may change. The governing principles established by this Charter shall remain controlling unless amended through the process defined herein.

This Charter establishes the highest internal authority for organizational philosophy, product development, strategic decision making, and the conduct of the Company.

## Article I - Scope

### Section 1. Applicability

This Charter governs:

1. Organizational philosophy.
2. Product philosophy.
3. Strategic decision making.
4. Research and experimentation.
5. Design and engineering standards.
6. Technology and artificial intelligence behavior.
7. Stewardship of user trust, attention, and information.

### Section 2. Limits

This Charter does not replace applicable law, regulation, contractual obligation, or professional duty.

This Charter does not govern the private beliefs of employees, contractors, partners, or users except where conduct affects Company work.

Operational procedures may be delegated to lower-level specifications, provided that no such specification conflicts with this Charter.

## Article II - Source

### Section 1. Source of Authority

This Charter is the primary source of philosophical and organizational authority within Qara Labs.

All Qara OS specifications, product documents, engineering standards, artificial intelligence instructions, and operating procedures shall remain consistent with this Charter.

Where a conflict exists, this Charter shall prevail.

### Section 2. Purpose of the Company

The Company exists to strengthen human understanding through products that remove unnecessary friction while respecting attention, autonomy, dignity, and trust.

The Company shall not pursue engagement, growth, novelty, or technological advancement as ends in themselves.

Growth and revenue shall be treated as evidence that value may have been created. They shall not define value by themselves.

### Section 3. Human Dignity

Every product, service, experiment, and internal process shall recognize the inherent dignity and agency of each person.

No product shall intentionally diminish autonomy, manipulate vulnerability, or create dependence for commercial advantage.

## Article III - Presence

### Section 1. Definition

Presence is the deliberate reduction of unnecessary cognitive, emotional, and technological noise so that individuals may devote greater attention to themselves, one another, and the matter before them.

Presence shall serve as a governing principle for Company decisions and product design.

### Section 2. Attention

Human attention shall be treated as finite and valuable.

Products shall minimize unnecessary interruption.

Products shall not use mechanisms designed solely to increase engagement where no corresponding user benefit exists.

### Section 3. Simplicity

Complexity shall require justification.

Every feature, interaction, workflow, and interface element shall demonstrate value proportional to the complexity it introduces.

Where two solutions produce substantially equivalent outcomes, the simpler solution shall be preferred.

### Section 4. Human Outcome

Technology shall remain subordinate to human experience.

Success shall be measured by the quality of the human outcome rather than the visibility, novelty, or sophistication of the technology.

## Article IV - Truth and Evidence

### Section 1. Belief

Every venture may begin with a belief.

Belief authorizes an experiment. It does not establish truth.

### Section 2. Evidence

Evidence shall be sought to test and refine beliefs, not to defend them.

Material assumptions shall be documented.

Low-confidence assumptions shall be tested before they govern significant commitments.

### Section 3. Revision

When reliable evidence consistently contradicts a belief, the belief shall change.

No person, including the Founder, shall be exempt from correction.

The Company shall remain committed to discovering what is true rather than preserving what it previously believed.

### Section 4. Venture Method

The Company shall use the following sequence for material product and venture decisions:

Truth -> Belief -> Experiment -> Evidence -> Decision.

## Article V - Stewardship

### Section 1. Duty

The Company and its representatives shall act as stewards of:

1. Human attention.
2. User trust.
3. Personal and organizational information.
4. Technological capability.
5. Knowledge created through Company work.
6. The principles established by this Charter.

### Section 2. Long-Term Responsibility

Decisions shall consider foreseeable long-term effects rather than short-term advantage alone.

Trust shall not be exchanged for growth, speed, or convenience without a documented and proportionate justification.

### Section 3. Accountability

Responsibility for judgment, ethics, and purpose remains human.

Delegation to software, automation, or artificial intelligence shall not remove human accountability.

## Article VI - Technology

### Section 1. Role

Technology is a tool used to accelerate understanding, reduce unnecessary work, and expand human capability.

Technology shall not be treated as purpose, identity, wisdom, or final authority.

### Section 2. Human Judgment

Technology may assist analysis, creation, communication, and execution.

Humans shall remain responsible for consequential decisions, ethical judgment, and the purposes for which technology is used.

### Section 3. Privacy and Security

Privacy and security shall be treated as foundational requirements where Company products handle personal, sensitive, or confidential information.

They shall not be deferred solely for speed of release.

### Section 4. Transparency

Products shall communicate material limitations, uncertainty, and automated behavior when such disclosure is necessary for informed use.

## Article VII - Products

### Section 1. Qualification

A Qara product shall identify:

1. The person or group it serves.
2. The unnecessary friction it removes.
3. The human outcome it seeks to improve.
4. The evidence needed to validate its value.
5. The risks it introduces.

### Section 2. Development

Products shall begin with the smallest responsible experiment capable of producing useful evidence.

The Company shall not add capability without a defined purpose.

### Section 3. Evaluation

A product shall be evaluated according to:

1. Lives improved.
2. Time returned.
3. Understanding strengthened.
4. Trust earned.
5. Human capability increased.
6. Evidence and learning accumulated.
7. Revenue generated as evidence of delivered value.

### Section 4. Revision or Retirement

A product shall be revised, paused, or retired when evidence shows that it no longer serves its stated purpose, materially conflicts with this Charter, or creates harm disproportionate to its benefit.

## Article VIII - Decisions

### Section 1. Required Questions

Every major decision shall answer:

1. What is the relevant truth or problem?
2. Who is served?
3. What evidence supports the decision?
4. Which Charter principles apply?
5. What tradeoffs and risks exist?
6. How will the decision be tested?
7. What result would require revision or reversal?

### Section 2. Authority of Evidence

Status, rank, confidence, tradition, and personal preference shall not outweigh relevant evidence.

Where evidence is incomplete, uncertainty shall be stated and the decision shall be proportionate to the available evidence.

### Section 3. Record

Material decisions shall preserve a concise record of the rationale, evidence, affected principles, and expected outcome.

## Article IX - The Founder and Stewards

### Section 1. Bound by the Charter

The Founder is bound by this Charter.

The Founder may not override a governing principle without following the amendment process.

### Section 2. Responsibility

The Founder's primary responsibility is to protect truth, purpose, and alignment rather than personal ownership of ideas.

The Founder shall remain willing to change direction when evidence requires it.

### Section 3. Identity and Ego

Success and failure shall be studied without allowing either to define the Founder or the Company.

The Company shall not preserve a strategy, product, or belief solely to protect the Founder's ego, reputation, or prior commitment.

### Section 4. Stewardship Beyond the Founder

Future leaders, employees, contractors, partners, and artificial intelligence agents operating on behalf of the Company shall act as stewards of this Charter within the scope of their authority.

## Article X - Amendment

### Section 1. Proposal

An amendment shall require a written proposal that identifies:

1. The language to be changed.
2. The reason for the change.
3. The evidence supporting the change.
4. The principles and subordinate documents affected.
5. The risks of adopting or rejecting the amendment.

### Section 2. Review

An amendment shall not be adopted solely for convenience, temporary market pressure, or preference.

The review shall determine whether the proposed change is necessary, durable, and consistent with the purpose of the Company.

### Section 3. Adoption

An adopted amendment shall receive:

1. A new Charter version.
2. A dated changelog entry.
3. A ratification record.
4. Updates to affected subordinate specifications.

### Section 4. Preservation

Superseded versions shall remain archived.

The history of the Charter shall not be erased.

## Founder's Promise

I will not become attached to my ideas.

I will become attached to discovering what is true.

I will allow evidence to change my mind.

I will build for people before I build for my ego.

I will remember that principles outlast tactics.

Technology changes. Truth does not.
